Message from CEO

Why Asia? An investor’s goal is to make money for which she needs growth and an Emerging Market does it better for you, even better than most of the developed countries.

Emerging economies are the engine of global growth! MSCI (Morgan Stanley Capital International) classifies 23 economies under the list of Emerging Markets based on the historical growth data of that country. Of all the emerging markets, there are 18 outperformers, who outperformed global benchmarks by achieving more than 3.5% per capita GDP growth over 50 years or 5% growth over the last 20 years. Out of these 18 outperformers, 15 countries are from Asia, which includes long-term success stories such as China and Malaysia, and recent high-growth economies such as India, South Korea, and Vietnam. No investor would want to miss on the train of $11 trillion, which would be added to the global economy by 2030, through the success of these emerging economies. On the other hand in the crypto world, crypto asset market capitalisation has increased from $25 billion in 2017 and $330 billion in 2018. Blockchain would be adding 10% to the world GDP in coming 5 years, hence we will have better ways to invest in growing market opportunities.

Why InvestoAsia? Practically in the current status quo, the Indian equity market is closed to most of the individual foreign investors. Before 2012, the Indian market was open only to registered foreign institutional investors (FII). Hence, an individual could only invest through Indian firms listed abroad by opening sub-accounts or in derivative markets through p-notes issued by FII, in return paying hefty fees. To encourage more investment, some rules were relaxed by Indian government post 2014 and foreign individuals can now theoretically invest directly. However, the process even today is bureaucratic on the broker’s side, appealing only to high net worth individuals because of high minima and no Indian firms yet seem to be offering effective services to overseas retail investors. Also, constraints like the difference in market operating hours, as well as prerequisite market knowledge adds to the investor’s pain points.

There are also few Indian firms listed internationally through ADRs and GDRs in the US and UK, but the overall exposure and quality is disappointing. The Indian Debt Market is even more frustrating for foreign individual investors, given the huge foreign appetite for rupee denominated debt as India gives the highest returns for its debt market.

Emerging Markets and Digital Assets; InvestoAsia brings best of both the worlds of International Investments and Blockchain Application. InvestoAsia is one of the most valuable applications of Internet 3.0, solves the real problem faced by every Retail Investor and will revolutionise the way investments are done into various assets classes, especially overseas.
